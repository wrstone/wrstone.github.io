Who I Am and How I Came To Be



I have led a somewhat eclectic and storied life:
  • I am a native-born South Dakotan.
  • I grew up in Lincoln, Nebraska.
  • I spent summers on my grandparents' cattle ranch in the ghost town of Pedro, South Dakota.
  • I failed as an actor.
  • I became a a successful computer scientist with two degrees in the field.
  • I acquired the monicker of "The Jackalope of Truth" during the several years that I taught IT at a junior collegee.
  • I spent ten years working in the Chicago metro area.
  • I spent another ten years in the Des Moines, Iowa metro area.
  • I spent about five years in the Sioux City, Iowa area.
  • Today, I've retired to the tourist trap of Wall, South Dakota, home of the world famous Wall Drug Store.
  • I own 3/5 of the Davis Homestead Reserve that includes my great-grandfather's pioneer log house. I'm very slowly renovating it to its original condition.
  • I run a pair of highly successful YouTube Channels:
    • SYL Ranch
    • SYL Ranch Darkroom

I've been called "the Ted McGinley of Information Technology" because for some reason, people tended to hire me when their company was in danger of total collapse. I like to think they hired me because they thought I could save the company.

Sometimes it worked. Sometimes it didn't.

When it didn't work, it was universally because the company had made the fatal mistake of putting the Marketing department in charge of the business.

Marketing is important, but when they drive the business, they inevitably drive it into the ground.

Bill Hicks On Marketing (Explicit Language)
